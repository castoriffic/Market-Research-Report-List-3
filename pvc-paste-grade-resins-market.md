<p><p>PVC paste grade resins are commonly produced using two main methods: the micro suspension method and the emulsion method. In the micro suspension method, the polymerization process occurs within a liquid medium, resulting in finely dispersed particles of PVC resin. On the other hand, the emulsion method involves the polymerization of PVC in an aqueous medium, forming a latex of fine PVC particles. Both methods offer unique advantages and are widely used in various industries for the manufacturing of PVC paste grade resins.</p></p>
