<p><p>Self-expanding metallic stents (SEMS) are used in medical procedures to treat blockages or strictures in various parts of the body. There are two main types of SEMS on the market: stainless steel and Nitinol. Stainless steel stents are rigid and provide excellent radial force, making them ideal for use in larger vessels. Nitinol stents, on the other hand, are more flexible and have shape memory properties, allowing them to conform to the shape of the vessel. Both types of stents have their own unique advantages and are used based on the specific needs of the patient.</p></p>
