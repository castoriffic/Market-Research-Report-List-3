<p><p>Secondary refrigerants are used as heat transfer fluids in cooling systems where direct contact with the primary refrigerant is not desired. Common types include glycols, such as propylene glycol and ethylene glycol, salt brines like calcium chloride or potassium acetate, carbon dioxide, and other fluids with specific properties for different applications. Glycols are versatile and commonly used in food and beverage industries, while salt brines are popular for low-temperature applications. Carbon dioxide is gaining popularity for its low environmental impact. Other secondary refrigerants include ammonia, hydrocarbons, and water-based solutions.</p></p>
