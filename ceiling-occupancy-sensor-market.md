<p><p>Ceiling occupancy sensors come in various types, with Passive Infrared (PIR) monitoring being one of the most common. PIR sensors detect changes in infrared energy in a room, such as movement by people. Ultrasonic monitoring sensors use sound waves to detect motion and occupancy in a space. Other types of ceiling occupancy sensors may include dual technology sensors, which combine PIR and ultrasonic technologies for more accurate detection. Each type of sensor offers unique benefits and is suited for different applications based on the monitoring requirements.</p></p>
