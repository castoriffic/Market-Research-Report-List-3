<p><p>The transportable ventilators market consists of two main types: invasive ventilation and non-invasive ventilation. Invasive ventilation utilizes a tube inserted into the patient's trachea to deliver oxygen into the lungs, commonly used in critical care settings. On the other hand, non-invasive ventilation provides respiratory support without the need for intubation. It includes methods such as masks or nasal prongs to deliver oxygen, commonly used to treat patients with respiratory disorders like sleep apnea or chronic obstructive pulmonary disease. Both types play crucial roles in assisting patients with breathing difficulties in various medical scenarios.</p></p>
