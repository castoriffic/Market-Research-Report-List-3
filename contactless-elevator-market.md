<p><p>A contactless elevator, also known as a touchless elevator, is an advanced technology that allows users to operate an elevator without the need for physically pressing buttons or touching any surfaces. Instead, this system uses gesture recognition, voice commands, or smartphone applications to control elevator functions, such as selecting floors or opening and closing doors.</p><p>The contactless elevator market has witnessed significant growth in recent years due to the increasing demand for hygiene and social distancing measures in public spaces, particularly in the wake of the COVID-19 pandemic. The market is expected to continue growing at a compound annual growth rate (CAGR) of 10.7% during the forecast period.</p><p>One of the key market trends is the integration of contactless elevator technology with artificial intelligence (AI) and Internet of Things (IoT) solutions. This enables these elevators to gather and analyze data, allowing for predictive maintenance and improved overall efficiency. Furthermore, the integration of AI and IoT technologies can enhance the user experience by providing personalized elevator settings and optimizing traffic flow.</p><p>Another trend in the contactless elevator market is the adoption of biometric recognition systems. These systems use fingerprint or facial recognition technology to identify and authorize elevator users, eliminating the need for physical touch or sensor-based activation. Biometric recognition not only enhances convenience and hygiene but also improves security by preventing unauthorized access.</p><p>Additionally, the contactless elevator market is witnessing increased demand from sectors such as healthcare, hospitality, and commercial buildings, where ensuring hygiene and minimizing contact is crucial. These industries are investing in touchless elevator solutions to provide a safe and seamless experience for their occupants.</p><p>In conclusion, the contactless elevator market is expected to experience robust growth in the coming years. The integration of AI and IoT technologies, the adoption of biometric recognition systems, and the increasing demand from various sectors are some of the key factors driving this market's expansion.</p></p>
