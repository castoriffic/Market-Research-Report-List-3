<p><p>Diffractive laser beamsplitters are classified into one-dimensional and two-dimensional markets based on their use and application. One-dimensional beamsplitters are designed to split incoming laser beams into two separate paths, commonly used in applications requiring precise beam splitting. On the other hand, two-dimensional beamsplitters are capable of splitting laser beams into multiple paths, making them suitable for more complex applications such as holography and microscopy. Both types of beamsplitters play a crucial role in various industries including telecommunications, healthcare, and research.</p></p>
