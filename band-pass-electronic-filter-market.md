<p><p>Band-pass electronic filters are categorized into two main types: passive and active electronic filters. </p><p>Passive electronic filters rely solely on passive components such as resistors, capacitors, and inductors to filter and manipulate signals within a specified frequency range. </p><p>In contrast, active electronic filters incorporate active components such as transistors or operational amplifiers to enhance and control the filtering process, offering greater flexibility and precision in signal manipulation. </p><p>Both types of filters play crucial roles in various applications, including telecommunications, audio processing, and instrumentation.</p></p>
