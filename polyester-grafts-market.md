<p><p>Polyester grafts, specifically knitted and woven vascular grafts, are commonly used in vascular surgery to repair or replace damaged blood vessels. Knitted polyester vascular grafts are made by interlocking loops of polyester fibers, providing flexibility and good handling during surgery. In contrast, woven polyester vascular grafts are created by weaving individual polyester fibers together, offering higher burst pressure resistance. Both types of polyester grafts are durable, biocompatible, and widely used in various vascular surgical procedures to restore blood flow and improve patient outcomes.</p></p>
