<p><p>Urinalysis test is conducted to analyze the urine for various medical purposes. Macroscopic urinalysis involves visual examination of urine's color, clarity, and odor, which can help detect abnormalities like bloody or cloudy urine. Biochemical urinalysis focuses on quantifying chemical components in urine, such as glucose, protein, or ketones, indicating conditions like diabetes or kidney disease. Both types of urinalysis tests play a crucial role in diagnosing and monitoring various health conditions by providing valuable insights into a patient's overall health and metabolic functions.</p></p>
