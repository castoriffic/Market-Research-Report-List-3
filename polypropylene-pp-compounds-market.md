<p><p>Polypropylene (PP) compounds are classified into two main types: homopolymer and copolymer. Homopolymer PP compounds are made from a single type of propylene monomer, resulting in a high level of purity and consistency. Copolymer PP compounds, on the other hand, are made by combining propylene with another monomer, which allows for additional properties such as increased impact resistance and clarity. Both homopolymer and copolymer PP compounds are widely used in various industries for their versatile performance and cost-effectiveness.</p></p>
