<p><p>Ferrous disulfide is available in two main market types: blocky and powder. Blocky ferrous disulfide is typically used in applications where a slower release of sulfur is needed, such as in the production of sulfur-containing chemicals. On the other hand, powder ferrous disulfide is preferred for applications requiring a faster release of sulfur, such as in the manufacturing of agricultural fertilizers. Both forms of ferrous disulfide offer distinct advantages depending on the specific needs of the end user.</p></p>
