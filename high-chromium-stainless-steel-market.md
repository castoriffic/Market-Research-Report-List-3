<p><p>High chromium stainless steel is a type of stainless steel alloy with a high chromium content, typically above 12%. This increases its resistance to corrosion and oxidation. There are four main types: Ferritic stainless steel, which is magnetic and has good resistance to stress corrosion cracking; Martensitic stainless steel, which is hard and strong but less corrosion-resistant; Austenitic stainless steel, which is non-magnetic and highly corrosion-resistant; and Precipitation hardened stainless steel, which has high strength and good corrosion resistance. Each type has its own unique properties and applications.</p></p>
