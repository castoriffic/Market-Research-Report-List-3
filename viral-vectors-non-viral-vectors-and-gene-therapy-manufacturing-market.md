<p><p>Viral vectors and non-viral vectors are two different technologies used in gene therapy. Gene therapy involves introducing genetic material into a patient's cells to treat or prevent diseases. Viral vectors refer to viruses that have been modified to carry therapeutic genes into targeted cells. These viruses are engineered to be safe and efficient delivery systems for gene therapy. Examples of viral vectors include retroviruses, lentiviruses, adenoviruses, and adeno-associated viruses.</p><p>On the other hand, non-viral vectors are non-viral delivery systems used in gene therapy. These vectors include various methods such as lipid-based nanoparticles, DNA nanoparticles, naked DNA, and electroporation. Non-viral vectors have advantages such as lower immunogenicity and lower manufacturing costs compared to viral vectors, but they generally have lower efficiency in delivering genes into targeted cells.</p><p>Gene therapy manufacturing involves the production and purification of viral vectors or non-viral vectors, as well as the production of therapeutic genes. This process requires strict adherence to quality standards, scalability, and regulatory requirements.</p><p>According to market growth analysis, the Viral Vectors, Non-Viral Vectors, and Gene Therapy Manufacturing Market is expected to experience significant growth during the forecast period.
