<p><p>Agricultural chelates market can be classified into various types based on the type of chelating agent used. Some common types include EDTA, EDDHA, DTPA, IDHA, and others. EDTA is widely used for micronutrient fertilizers due to its strong chelating properties. EDDHA is preferred for iron chelation in alkaline soils. DTPA is commonly used for micronutrient fertilization in acid soils. IDHA is known for its high stability and effectiveness in chelating micronutrients. Other types include HEDTA, EDDHSA, and HEDP.</p></p>

<p><p>Agricultural chelates are used in various applications within the agriculture industry. Soil application involves adding chelates directly to the soil to improve nutrient uptake by plants. Seed dressing involves coating seeds with chelates to enhance germination and early plant growth. Foliar sprays are used to deliver chelates directly to plant leaves for quick absorption. Fertigation involves mixing chelates with irrigation water to deliver nutrients to plants through the roots. Other applications of agricultural chelates include hydroponics and greenhouse cultivation.</p></p>
